About Me

My journey from engineering to data science
TV Design Engineer | SONY
Backlight System Engineer

After graduating, I began my career at Sony as a Design Engineer in the TV Design - Backlight System team for 3 years. I contributed to frame dimming and local dimming projects for the BRAVIA X95H and X75 series. I designed and evaluated backlight control specifications, covering the TV user interface, sensors, power sequences, motion improvements, and picture quality across Prototype, DVT, and PVT stages.

I prepared parameters for brightness control, local dimming profiles, peak brightness sustainability, MotionFlow XR settings, and VRR, ensuring adherence to Sony's standards. My expertise in analyzing and debugging backlight issues led to effective solutions and comprehensive reports tracked through JIRA. Additionally, I prepared check-sheets and conducted FMEA for evaluation and testing.

I managed ODM TV models by collaborating with Taiwanese vendors and Chinese factories, ensuring seamless integration and production efficiency. I also facilitated meetings for schedule planning, issue resolution, reverse engineering, and continuous improvement, driving innovation and precision in Sony's TV design projects.

LCD Production Engineer | AUO
New Product Introduction Engineer | Photolithography Track Process Engineer 


At AUO, I held two key roles, demonstrating my expertise in new product introduction and process engineering.

For nearly a year as an Array New Product Introduction Engineer, I integrated my understanding of LCD array processes, including photolithography, CVD, PVD, and etching, utilizing SPC to set specifications and control limits. I led two mass productions within 28-day cycles, achieving yields over 95% during the DVT phase. I evaluated emerging display technologies, including LTPS fingerprint sensors, and refined processes for new materials such as photoresists, black metals, and CVD layers. Collaborating with R&D and Project Teams, I worked on new product evaluation, design review, and issue debugging, conducting project kick-off meetings to streamline handover and communication. I also led DVT issue investigations and improvements, conducting Failure Mode Analysis (FMA) with R&D, which significantly improved production yield.

After that, I transitioned to the role of Photolithography Track Process Engineer, a position I held for nearly a year. In this role, I managed and optimized photo track processes to maximize yield. I provided troubleshooting support and facilitated lot disposition to address manufacturing issues promptly. I conducted root cause analysis for process deviations, implemented corrective actions, and performed equipment maintenance, calibration, and troubleshooting to maintain efficiency, minimize downtime, and ensure smooth operations. Additionally, I maintained comprehensive documentation for photo track processes, provided technical support to production teams, and conducted visual defect inspections. I recorded findings and retrained the AOI model with correctly labeled defect modes to improve accuracy.

Data Science Student | General Assembly
Computer Vision Enthusiast

After my time at AUO, I pursued my long-standing interest in computer vision. Throughout my career, I frequently took online courses to deepen my understanding of this field, which continuously sparked my interest. When AUO decided to close its Singapore production lines, I saw it as an opportunity to make a significant career switch.

I decided to attend the General Assembly (GA) Data Science Immersive (DSI) bootcamp to equip myself with the necessary knowledge and skills in data science. This 12-week intensive learning journey provided me with a comprehensive understanding of data science concepts and practical applications. I successfully completed the bootcamp, further fueling my passion for computer vision and data-driven problem-solving.
