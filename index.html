About Me

My journey from engineering to data science
TV Design Engineer | SONY
Backlight System Engineer

I started my career at Sony as a Design Engineer in the TV Design - Backlight System team. I contributed to the BRAVIA X95H and X75 series, focusing on frame dimming and local dimming projects. I designed backlight control specifications, developed parameters for brightness control, and debugged backlight issues. I managed ODM TV models, collaborating with overseas vendors and factory, and conducted FMEA for testing. This role honed my skills in technical problem-solving, data analysis, and cross-functional collaboration, applicable to data science and AI engineering.

LCD Production Engineer | AUO
New Product Introduction Engineer | Photolithography Track Process Engineer 


At AUO, I held two key roles, demonstrating my expertise in new product introduction and process engineering.

As an Array New Product Introduction Engineer, I integrated LCD array processes like photolithography, CVD, PVD, and etching, using SPC to set specifications. I led two mass productions within 28-day cycles, achieving yields over 95% during the DVT phase. I evaluated new display technologies, refined processes for new materials, and collaborated with R&D and Project Teams on product evaluation, design review, and issue debugging.

Later, as a Photolithography Track Process Engineer, I managed and optimized photo track processes to maximize yield. I provided troubleshooting support, conducted root cause analysis, implemented corrective actions, and maintained comprehensive documentation. I also supported production teams and conducted visual defect inspections, retraining the AOI model to improve accuracy.

Data Science Student | General Assembly
Computer Vision Enthusiast

After my time at AUO, I pursued my long-standing interest in computer vision. Throughout my career, I frequently took online courses to deepen my understanding of this field, which continuously sparked my interest. When AUO decided to close its Singapore production lines, I saw it as an opportunity to make a significant career switch.

I decided to attend the General Assembly (GA) Data Science Immersive (DSI) bootcamp to equip myself with the necessary knowledge and skills in data science. This 12-week intensive learning journey provided me with a comprehensive understanding of data science concepts and practical applications. I successfully completed the bootcamp, further fueling my passion for computer vision and data-driven problem-solving.

My Projects

My projects during my 12 weeks at General Assembly focused on data analysis, machine learning, and computer vision, applying theoretical knowledge to real-world problems.
TheiaVision: Object Detection Technology for PMD Safety Alerts

Developed a computer vision model to enhance the safety of Personal Mobility Devices (PMDs) in urban environments using self-annotated custom datasets of vehicles, pedestrians, and traffic signs with YOLOv8 object detection technology and transfer learning, achieving an 80.5% recall rate.

Skin Cancer Detection with Convolutional Neural Networks

Developed a Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN) model and Streamlit app to visually distinguish between benign and malignant skin cancer spots, improving visual tests and reducing the need for invasive biopsies, with an 89% accuracy rate.

Classifying Customer Feedback for Grab's Google Play Store Reviews

Developed a Natural language processing (NLP) model to classify customer feedback from Grab's Google Play Store reviews into ride-hailing and delivery services using data webscraped from Reddit’s Uber and UberEats subreddits, achieving an 82.37% accuracy rate and providing actionable insights for product improvements. Applied sentiment analysis to assess customer satisfaction across other platforms. Additionally, developed a Streamlit app capable of automatically classifying CSV files with a large number of reviews.

Predicting HDB Resale Values for Young and Expecting Families

Developed a predictive model to estimate HDB resale values using features like proximity to amenities, distance to CBD, and primary school tiers. Tested models included Linear Regression, Ridge, and Lasso regression. Conducted data preprocessing, feature engineering, and model evaluation to provide accurate property valuations for homebuyers.
